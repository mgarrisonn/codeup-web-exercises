National Park FAQs

Display Info

Big Bend
Named for the prominent bend in the Rio Grande along the U.S.-Mexico border, this park encompasses a large remote part of the Chihuahuan Desert.

Grand Canyon
The Grand Canyon, carved by the might Colorado River, is 277 miles long, up to 1 mile deep, and up to 15 miles wide.

Yellow Stone
Situated on the Yellowstone Caldera, the park has an expansive network of geothermal area including boiling mud pots, vividly coloured hot springs such as Grand Prismatic.

Badlands
The Badlands are a collection of buttes, pinnacles, spires, and mixed-grass prairies.

American Samoa
The southernmost national park is on three Samoan islands and protects coral reefs, rainforests, volcanic mountains, and white beaches.

Biscayne
Located in Biscayne Bay, this park at the north end of the Florida Keys has four interrelated marine ecosystems: mangove forest, the Bay, the Keys, and coral reefs.

Crater Lake
Crater Lake lies in the caldera of an ancient volcano called Mount Mazama that collapsed 7,700 years ago

Death Valley
Death Valley is the hottest, lowest, and driest place in the United Sates, with daytime temperatures that have exceeded 130 degrees F.

Everglades
The Everglades are the largest tropical wilderness in the United Sates.

Great Smokey Mountains
The Great Smokey Mountains, part of the Appalachian Mountains, span a wide range of elevations, making them home to over 400 vertebrate species, 100 tree species, and 5000 plant species.

Yosemite

  • Location: Mariposa, California
  • Area of the park: 748,436 acres
  • Established: October 1, 1890
  • Visitors in 2019: 4,009,436

White Sands

  • Location: Alamogordo, New Mexico
  • Area of the park: 145,762 acres
  • Established: January 18, 1933
  • Visitors in 2019: 608,785

Saguaro

  • Located: Tucson, Arizona
  • Area of the park: 91,716 acres
  • Established: October 14, 1994
  • Named after the saguaro cactus
